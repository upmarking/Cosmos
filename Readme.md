Build a complete mobile and web app for curated professional networking and founder matchmaking. The product should help users discover the right people, meet in a structured way, keep conversations organized, and grow valuable professional relationships over time.

This is not a job board, not a sales platform, and not a generic social network. It is a high-trust relationship building platform for founders, operators, professionals, investors, students, mentors, service providers, and other career oriented users.

The experience should feel premium, simple, intentional, and highly organized.

PRIMARY PRODUCT GOAL
Create a platform where users can:
1. Discover and match with relevant people
2. Join curated events and networking sessions
3. Maintain structured conversations and follow-ups
4. Join or create communities
5. Track relationship progress and networking outcomes
6. Build trust through profiles, mutual connections, LinkedIn based import, and peer endorsements
7. Use AI to summarize meetings, surface next steps, and improve follow-up quality

CORE PRODUCT PRINCIPLES
1. Quality over quantity
   Limit users to a maximum of 10 meaningful new connections per month to keep networking intentional and valuable.

2. Trust first
   Profiles should feel credible, professional, and verifiable. LinkedIn connection should be used as a trust signal and profile enrichment source.

3. Purpose driven connections
   Every match should be based on goals, tags, interests, and mutual relevance, not random discovery.

4. Structured communication
   Conversations should behave like a relationship CRM, where people can organize chats, goals, labels, notes, meeting summaries, and next steps.

5. Event driven networking
   Networking events should be scheduled, structured, and timed, with clear rounds and feedback loops.

6. Community led growth
   Communities should be curated, admin created, and led by designated managers.

7. AI assisted productivity
   AI should summarize meetings, create action points, help with follow ups, and surface useful patterns from interactions.

1. Connect
2. Events
3. Communities
4. Conversations
5. Profile
6. Notifications

ONBOARDING FLOW
Create a smooth onboarding flow that collects the following:

1. Basic identity
   Full name, profile photo, headline, location, current role, company, years of experience

2. Professional intent
   What they are looking for on the platform, such as
   - Co founders
   - Business partners
   - Mentors
   - Collaborators
   - Investors
   - Clients
   - Hiring opportunities
   - Strategic introductions
   - Industry peers
   - Community support

3. Interest tags
   Users should select tags such as startup, AI, marketing, product, design, finance, legal, healthcare, education, mobility, consumer, SaaS, web3, content, sales, growth, operations, HR, and more.

4. Goal statement
   A short statement about what they want to achieve on the platform.

5. Long term vision
   A longer statement that describes what they are building, why they are here, and what kind of people they want to meet.

6. Availability preferences
   Preferred meeting format, preferred time windows, meeting duration preferences, and event participation preferences.

7. LinkedIn connection
   Give users the option to connect their LinkedIn account. Use this to import relevant profile details such as current role, company, experience, skills, and professional background. Show that the account is linked to LinkedIn as a trust signal. Do not claim any access to an internal LinkedIn verified badge. The app can display that the user has connected LinkedIn, but not a third party verified badge.

8. Profile completion nudges
   Show a progress indicator encouraging users to complete their profile before unlocking full discovery.

CONNECT SECTION
This is the core matchmaking area.

Create a swipe based discovery engine where users see profiles one by one.

Each profile card should show:
1. Name
2. Profile photo
3. Headline
4. Current role and company
5. Primary tags
6. Goal statement
7. Short bio
8. Mutual connections count
9. LinkedIn linked status if connected
10. Top endorsed skills
11. Any relevant badges or trust indicators inside the platform

Matching rules:
1. Users only see profiles that match their selected interests, goals, and tags.
2. Discovery should prioritize relevance over randomness.
3. Mutual connections should increase ranking.
4. LinkedIn connected profiles should be ranked higher.
5. People who clearly do not fit the user’s interests should not be shown.
6. Enforce the monthly limit of 10 meaningful connections.
7. Meaningful connection should be defined as a mutual match that opens a conversation or a meeting flow.

Swiping behavior:
1. Swipe right or tap yes to express interest
2. Swipe left or tap no to skip
3. If both users express interest, a match is created
4. After a match, the app should immediately move the users into Conversations
5. Matched users should not exchange direct contact information by default
6. The app should be the primary place for communication and follow up

Connection quality controls:
1. Show why the match is recommended
2. Show shared tags or shared goals
3. Show mutual connections
4. Keep the interface simple and premium, not cluttered

EVENTS SECTION
Create a structured networking event system.

Event types:
1. Open networking sessions
2. Curated founder meetups
3. Industry specific networking rounds
4. Invite only sessions
5. Themed events based on goals or interests

Event structure:
1. Users can register for events
2. Each event can contain multiple 15 minute rounds
3. Participants can prioritize who they want to meet
4. The system should schedule meetings based on preferences and availability
5. There should be a clear event flow from registration to matching to meetings to feedback

Paid event flow:
1. The platform can charge a fee upfront for event participation
2. Attendance should be encouraged through the upfront commitment
3. After the event, users rate the quality of conversations
4. Refund logic can be based on ratings, where better rated experiences receive better refunds
5. The app should clearly explain the refund logic before payment

During events:
1. Show meeting schedule
2. Show who each user is meeting next
3. Show meeting countdowns
4. Show event agenda
5. Show post meeting feedback prompts

After events:
1. Prompt users to rate the interaction
2. Prompt users to save follow up goals
3. Generate AI meeting summaries
4. Store summaries in the relevant conversation thread

CONVERSATIONS SECTION
This should work like a relationship CRM, but presented in a simple, user friendly way.

Each matched connection should have a dedicated conversation thread.

Conversation features:
1. One to one chat
2. Meeting transcript storage
3. AI generated summary after each meeting
4. Key discussion points
5. Decisions made
6. Next steps
7. Follow up reminders
8. Labels or tags for each conversation
9. Private goals for each connection
10. Conversation notes visible only to the user who created them
11. Attachments or shared resources if needed

Conversation labels should include examples such as:
1. Potential partner
2. Follow up needed
3. Warm intro requested
4. Collaboration opportunity
5. Meeting completed
6. Important contact
7. Active discussion
8. Needs response

Private goals in conversations:
1. Users can set a private goal for each connection
2. The other person should not see the private goal
3. Example goals include collaboration, intro request, mentoring, partnership, hiring, or learning more about the person
4. The system should help users track whether the goal is moving forward

AI meeting summaries:
1. Generate a concise summary after each meeting
2. Include important topics discussed
3. Include action items
4. Include next steps
5. Include open questions
6. Include follow up reminders
7. Store the summary in the conversation thread
8. Allow users to edit or delete summaries if needed

Warm introduction flow:
1. If a user wants to meet someone through a mutual connection, they can request a warm introduction
2. Show shared connections clearly
3. Let users request introductions in a respectful way
4. Track intro requests and responses
5. Show intro status inside the conversation

ENDORSEMENTS
Add a peer endorsement system for skills and strengths.

How it should work:
1. Users can endorse specific skills on another user’s profile
2. Endorsements should appear as star counts or similar visual indicators
3. Tapping a skill should reveal who endorsed it
4. Endorsements should be lightweight and simple like a quick tap
5. Only relevant professional skills should be endorsable
6. Examples include communication, product thinking, fundraising, design, sales, leadership, hiring, strategy, operations, and technical ability

MATCH QUALITY AND RECOMMENDATION LOGIC
The app should recommend people based on:
1. Shared tags
2. Complementary goals
3. Mutual relevance
4. Industry alignment
5. Role fit
6. Geographic proximity if relevant
7. LinkedIn connected status
8. Mutual connections
9. Previous interaction quality
10. Event participation history

Make the app feel intelligent, not random.

RULES FOR PLATFORM CULTURE
This is very important.

1. The platform is for relationship building, not direct selling
2. Do not allow users to spam pitches
3. Do not allow users to treat the platform like a cold outreach database
4. If someone is repeatedly reported for unsolicited selling behavior, the platform should warn, restrict, or limit their visibility
5. Onboarding should clearly explain the culture and purpose of the platform
6. The tone should encourage meaningful connection, trust, and collaboration
